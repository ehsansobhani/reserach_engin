## Abstract

 Optimized charging of electric vehicles (EVs) at public locations consists of two decisions: how much energy to deliver at what times, which is continuous, and where to plug in, which is binary. This makes optimizing EV charging a mixed-integer linear program (MILP). This discreteness undermines traditional marginal pricing methods.
