## Abstract

 The growth in Electric Vehicle (EV) market share is expected to increase power demand on distribution networks. Uncoordinated residential EV charging, based on driving routines, creates peak demand at various zone substations depending on location and time. Leveraging smart charge scheduling and Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) technologies offers opportunities to adjust charge schedules, allowing for load shifting and grid support, which can reduce both charging costs and grid stress.
